# Lotto Max Game Rules & Prize Structure

## Overview

Lotto Max is Canada's largest national lottery game, administered by the Interprovincial Lottery Corporation (ILC) and offered through provincial lottery corporations across all Canadian provinces and territories. Launched in 2009, it replaced the original Lotto Super 7 game.

**Important Update (May 2026)**: The number pool has been expanded from 1-50 to **1-52**, changing the odds and probability calculations.

## Game Mechanics

### Number Selection
- **Main Numbers**: Players select 7 numbers from a pool of **1 to 52**
- **Draw Format**: Each draw selects 7 main numbers plus 1 bonus number
- **Selection Method**: Mechanical ball machine with air mixing

### Draw Schedule
- **Frequency**: Twice weekly
- **Days**: Tuesday and Friday evenings
- **Time**: Approximately 10:30 PM Eastern Time
- **Ticket Cutoff**: Typically 10:30 PM ET on draw nights

### Ticket Cost and Format
- **Cost**: $6 per play
- **Included Lines**: Each $6 purchase includes 3 sets of 7 numbers
- **Selection Options**:
  - Quick Pick (computer-generated random selection)
  - Selection Slip (player-chosen numbers)
  - Combination Play (systematic coverage)

### Overall Odds
- **Any prize**: approximately 1 in 167

### Jackpot Rules
- **Minimum Jackpot**: $10,000,000
- **Maximum Jackpot**: $80,000,000 (capped)
- **Jackpot Growth**: When not won, jackpot grows by approximately $2-5 million per draw
- **Rollover**: If jackpot not won, entire amount rolls to next draw
- **Shared Jackpot**: If multiple winners, jackpot is split equally

### MaxMillions and MaxPlus
When the jackpot reaches certain thresholds, additional prizes are offered:
- **MaxMillions**: $1,000,000 prizes when jackpot exceeds $50M
- **MaxPlus**: Additional draws with guaranteed prizes at higher jackpot levels
- **Carryover**: Unwon MaxMillions prizes can carry forward

## Combination Play Options

### System Play
Players can select more than 7 numbers to generate multiple combinations:
- **System 8**: Select 8 numbers = 8 combinations
- **System 9**: Select 9 numbers = 36 combinations
- **System 10**: Select 10 numbers = 120 combinations
- **System 11**: Select 11 numbers = 330 combinations
- **System 12**: Select 12 numbers = 792 combinations

## Historical Context

### Game Evolution
- **2009**: Lotto Max launched, replacing Lotto Super 7 (pool 1-49)
- **2019**: Number pool expanded from 49 to 50
- **2024**: Jackpot cap raised from $70M to $80M
- **May 2026**: Number pool expanded from 50 to **52**

### Impact of Pool Expansion (50 → 52)
- Total combinations increased from C(50,7)=99,884,400 to C(52,7)=133,784,560
- Jackpot odds increased from 1 in 33,294,800 to approximately 1 in 44,594,853 per $6 play
- Each number's individual probability decreased from 14.0% to 13.5%
- Expected sum of 7 numbers increased from 178.5 to 185.5

## Probability Mathematics

### Total Possible Combinations
- **Formula**: C(52,7) = 52! / (7! × 45!)
- **Total**: 133,784,560 unique combinations
- **Jackpot Odds per $6 play** (3 lines): approximately 1 in 44,594,853

### Expected Value
The expected value of a Lotto Max ticket depends on the jackpot size:
- At $10M jackpot: EV is strongly negative per $6 ticket
- At $50M jackpot: EV improves but remains negative per $6 ticket
- At $80M jackpot: EV approaches breakeven but typically remains negative

Even at maximum jackpot, the expected value remains negative because:
1. Only a fraction of ticket sales fund prizes (approximately 48%)
2. Jackpot sharing reduces individual expected value
3. The larger pool (52) further reduces individual odds

## Responsible Play Guidelines

Lotto Max is designed as entertainment. Key responsible play principles:
1. Set a budget and stick to it
2. Never chase losses
3. Don't borrow money to play
4. Play for fun, not as an investment strategy
5. Know when to stop
6. Each draw is independent - past results don't affect future outcomes
7. The pool expansion to 52 makes winning even harder statistically
